pookodelake

When you are shortlisting Wayanad’s sightseeing places, remember to include Pookode or Pookot Lake in your itinerary. This beautiful freshwater lake is nestled among the verdant forests of the Western Ghats. The lake’s shape is interesting because it resembles India’s physical map.

soochiparafalls

The three-tiered Soochipara Falls in Vellarimala, also known as Sentinel Rock Waterfalls, is a beautiful place in Wayanad. The sight of large volumes of water cascading from a height of 656-feet among lush green forests and massive rocks is a visual treat! Apart from spending some tranquil time near the falls, you can also take a bath or swim in the pool below and go rock climbing.

meenmutti

Meenmutty Falls, a wonderful retreat in the lap of nature, is a must-visit Wayanad tourist place. This three-tiered waterfall is 984-feet tall and surrounded by verdant green forests. To reach this natural wonder, one must trek for about 2 km through the forests. The waterfall has different viewing decks and the sight from the summit is incomparable! Shutterbugs must carry their cameras to capture the enticing views.

chembra peak

Chembra is the highest peak of the Wayanad Hill Range, rising 6890-feet above sea level. The place boasts a lush green and soothing ambience that draws nature lovers from all over. Adventurers can enjoy a thrilling trek up to the peak while soaking in the beauty of the surroundings. Major attractions here are a heart-shaped lake on the way to the peak and panoramic views of Banasura Dam and tea plantations from above.

edakkal caves

Edakkal Caves are the only structures in India with about 8000-year-old carvings that date back to the Neolithic Era or the late Stone Age. The two caves are located on Ambukuthi Hills, 3937-feet above sea level. As a result, one must trek for approximately 2 hours before reaching the entrance. Apart from tourists, history buffs and archaeology students interested in studying the carvings of human figures and animals visit these mysterious caves.
